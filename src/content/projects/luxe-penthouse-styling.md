This King West penthouse had floor-to-ceiling windows and panoramic city views, but the interiors felt cold and disconnected. The owner — a young tech executive with a passion for contemporary art — wanted a space that felt like a private gallery and a warm home in equal measure.

We anchored each room with a statement art piece, then built the surrounding design language around it. The living room's oversized abstract canvas in deep navy and gold informed the entire apartment's palette. A custom sectional in rich midnight velvet, brass accent tables, and a hand-tufted rug in charcoal and cream created a sophisticated seating arrangement worthy of the art it framed.

The primary bedroom became a sanctuary of layered textures — a performance linen headboard, silk throw pillows in muted plum, and blackout drapery in the same midnight tone as the living room, ensuring visual cohesion throughout the home.

The dining area was reimagined as both a functional space and a showcase — a marble-topped oval table surrounded by sculptural chairs, lit by a contemporary linear chandelier in brushed gold. Floor-to-ceiling bookshelves filled with curated objects, photography books, and small sculptural pieces transformed the adjacent hallway into a gallery corridor.
