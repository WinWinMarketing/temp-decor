Jennifer and Thomas chose Muskoka's lakeside pavilion for its natural beauty — crystal-clear water, towering pines, and dramatic Canadian sunsets. Our challenge was to enhance without competing with one of nature's most stunning backdrops.

The ceremony site featured a minimalist wooden arch — a clean, geometric frame that drew the eye to the lake beyond while providing just enough structure for trailing greenery and white peonies. Guests sat in whitewashed wooden chairs along a petal-strewn aisle of soft blue and cream.

The reception tent was an open-air pavilion draped in sheer white fabric that billowed gently in the lakeside breeze. Long family-style tables replaced traditional rounds, creating an intimate, communal dining experience. Each table ran the full length with a continuous garland of eucalyptus, dusty miller, and white ranunculus.

Place settings featured sea-glass-colored glassware, natural linen napkins, and hand-calligraphed name cards on smooth lake stones. As the sun set, hundreds of string lights woven through the pavilion's wooden beams created a canopy of warm light above the celebrating guests.
