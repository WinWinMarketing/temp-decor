When one of Toronto's top financial firms commissioned their annual holiday gala, they wanted an experience that felt transformative — something that would make their teams forget the office and step into a winter wonderland.

The Fairmont Royal York's grand ballroom was completely reimagined. The entrance featured a 15-foot tunnel of frosted birch branches with thousands of tiny LED lights, creating the sensation of walking through an enchanted winter forest. Dry-ice fog machines added a mystical ground-level mist.

Inside, the palette was silver, ice blue, and pristine white. Towering centerpieces of white branches spray-painted silver and draped with crystal beads reflected the ice-blue uplighting, creating a shimmering, ethereal atmosphere. Mercury glass votives by the hundreds added warmth to the cool palette.

The stage featured a custom backdrop of cascading white fabric panels backlit in gradient blues, framing the evening's live entertainment. A dessert station disguised as an ice palace — complete with carved ice sculptures and mirrored surfaces — became the most photographed element of the evening.
