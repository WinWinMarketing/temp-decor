Maya and Daniel wanted their wedding to feel like it emerged naturally from the gardens themselves. Rather than imposing a design onto the landscape, we worked with the existing botanical beauty — amplifying what nature had already provided.

The ceremony arch was a living structure, woven from willow branches and bursting with garden roses, jasmine, and trailing ivy. It framed the couple perfectly against the backdrop of the setting sun and the garden's ancient oak trees.

Each guest chair was adorned with a single sprig of lavender and a ribbon in sage green, filling the air with a gentle fragrance as guests took their seats. The aisle featured scattered petals from the same rose varieties used in the bridal bouquet, creating a visual thread that connected every element.

As twilight settled, hundreds of candles in glass hurricanes illuminated the pathways between the ceremony and reception spaces. The transition felt magical — guests wandered through candlelit garden paths, discovering small vignette arrangements of flowers and vintage books at every turn.
