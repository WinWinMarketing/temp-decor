Ananya and Vikram's wedding was a three-day celebration that honored their families' traditions while incorporating contemporary design elements. The couple wanted their ceremony to feel authentically rooted in culture yet refreshingly modern.

The mandap was the centerpiece — a four-pillar structure draped in deep sapphire blue fabric with gold embroidered borders, topped with an abundance of marigolds, red roses, and jasmine garlands. Crystal elements added contemporary sparkle to the traditional structure, creating a fusion of old and new.

Each event in the three-day celebration had its own distinct palette and personality. The mehndi night featured a bohemian garden setup with low seating, colorful cushions, and hanging lanterns in amber and turquoise. The sangeet transformed the hall into a Bollywood-inspired dance floor with LED lighting, mirror balls, and a custom neon sign of the couple's names.

The reception balanced the rich jewel tones with elegant restraint — tall centerpieces of red and burgundy roses in gold mercury vases, table settings with charger plates in deep navy, and hundreds of tea lights creating intimate warmth throughout the 300-guest celebration.
